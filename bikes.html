In July 2018, I went to Whistler, B.C.

We drove up to Whistler from Vancouver in a rental car.
Upon arrival, the first thing I noticed was a sea of mountain bikers.
I had no idea who these foreign people were. Riding a red cruiser
bike around Stanley Park in Vancouver was my first time riding
a bike in at least 2 years. I watched as the mountain bikers charged
down their dirt descents in full face helmets. My mom said
something along the lines of "that looks really dangerous."
Unbeknownst to me, I was at one of the world's premier bike
parks... and I hiked the whole time.


Within a week of returning to America, I got my first mountain
bike: a used 2013 Haro Flightline One. It retailed at $330 and
I got it for $115 on Craigslist. I learned basic skills on the Haro
and crashed a good bit, too. This bike served me well until
September 2018, when I took a trip to North Carolina's Dupont
State Forest. My brother, dad, and I rented a Santa Cruz
Chameleon from a Brevard bike shop and hit the trails. I rode
five miles on the Chameleon. Then I switched with my brother:
he rode the Chameleon for five miles while I rode his 2005 Giant
Boulder SE. The difference smacked me in the face. We rode
Ridgeline, the famous flow trail, two times. I used the Chameleon
the first time, and it was a blast. I used the Boulder the second time,
and I almost died. I felt every bump, in stark contrast to the
Chameleon and its Fox Rhythm 34 suspension fork.


On October 20th, 2018, after multiple months of searching
Craigslist and Facebook Marketplace, I got my "dream bike:"
the 2018 Kona Honzo AL/DR.
